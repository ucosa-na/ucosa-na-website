About UCOSA

Ugbeka College Old Students' Association is a not-for-profit organization whose members are alumni/alumnus of Ugbeka College Evboehighae. It has world-wide affiliations, namely: UCOSA-North America, UCOSA-Benin City, UCOSA-Europe, etc.

In particular, the UCOSA-North America members are from the U.S. and Canada only.

Who Can Become A Member

a.) Membership

Membership is open to all former students of Ugbeka College Evboehighae residing in North America, irrespective of the period of their candidature in the school. This means that all former students of Ugbeka College residing in the U.S. or Canada are automatically members. No membership registration fee of any sort is required.

b.) How Do I Get On Board?

If you attended Ugbeka College for any number of years, whether you graduated from the school or not, you are potentially a member. Just reach out to any UCOSA-NA executive officer — the President, P.R.O or Secretary. Your full names, phone number and email are enough to bring you back to your old friends and brothers and sisters again.

Aims And Objectives

The purpose of the association includes, but is not limited to, the following:

  1. To be a not-for-profit, non-political, charitable, and voluntary organization.
  2. To help old students maintain the ties of friendship.
  3. To identify and organize alumni of UCOSA in the Americas.
  4. To promote the welfare of its members and provide support as necessary.
  5. To help to strengthen teaching and learning at our alma mater.
  6. To support and collaborate in any viable projects and programs as may be initiated by the school's administration, benevolent organizations, Parent Teachers Association (PTA), school board of governors and other chapters of UCOSA to salvage, sustain, uphold, promote and protect the image of our alma mater.
  7. To promote any other activities that may be deemed necessary and appropriate in the interest of the association.

What Is The Association's Revenue Source

Revenue sources of UCOSA comprise, but are not limited to, the following:

  1. Members' annual dues of $100 US per member per year.
  2. Incidental levies/voluntary contributions as may be deemed necessary by the executive branch and the general membership assembly.
  3. Donations by individuals and government/private entities.

How Do Members Meet And Engage

Members conduct meetings via Zoom video conferencing app. Members easily connect to meeting sessions using any internet connected device: Desktop, Laptop, iPad, Tablets and Smartphones.

Information exchange between members and collaboration on the various committees' assignments happen on the UCOSA-North America general WhatsApp platform and the committee specific WhatsApp platforms. Information is also shared through the general UCOSA email. All members receive messages sent to:

ucosa.northamerica@gmail.com

Meeting Day And Time

The General Assembly meetings are held once every two months, on the last Sunday of the meeting month. Meeting time is 5:00 p.m. Eastern Standard Time.
